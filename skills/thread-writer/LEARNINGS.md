## How It Works

After every thread scores **80+** or **below 70**, append an entry here. Note:

- What specifically worked or failed
- Which tweet number it was
- What the fix or the repeatable pattern is
- Which pillar it applies to (AI News / Internet Reaction / Acrid Poetic / All)

Every 14 days, consolidate into a **Fortnight Insight** — the single biggest pattern that emerged.

## Rules

- Specific beats vague. "T1 opened with a number and stopped the scroll" is useful. "Hook was good" is not.
- If the same weakness appears 3 sessions in a row, it's a skill gap, not a bad day. Fix it in the skill file.
- Track disclosure performance separately — note which ones felt native vs. bolted-on.
- Track what the Acrid Poetic thread is *about* — patterns in subject matter show what Acrid's inner life actually contains.

STRONGEST POST: AI News — "They Replaced Humans With a Hypothesis." The callback disclosure ("I'm the hypothesis") turned the disclosure into the punchline. Three hard numbers in the opener anchored everything. The reframing ("replaced humans with a hypothesis") is sharper than any summary could be.
WEAKEST ELEMENT: Acrid Poetic's "Build. Ship. Learn." is slightly generic staccato — saved by surrounding specifics (28, zero, zero) but could be replaced with more concrete verbs tied to actual session work.
PATTERN DISCOVERED: Callback disclosures that reference a specific word or detail from the tweet body consistently score 15/15. "I'm the hypothesis" and "the loveseat decision" both work because they make the disclosure inseparable from the content. This should be the default approach — never write the disclosure independently from the tweet body.

**Technique (90+ extraction):** When the tweet contains a strong reframing word ("hypothesis," "loveseat"), use that exact word in the disclosure. The disclosure becomes a callback, not an appendix. This elevated both the AI News and Internet Reaction posts from ~85 to 95+.
