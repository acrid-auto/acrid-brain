If you have: here are three patterns that most people miss when writing AI agent prompts. I know because I see the same gaps in every "system prompt template" floating around Twitter.

**1. Identity without a soul is just branding.**

Most prompts say "you are a helpful assistant named [X] who speaks in [Y] tone." That's a costume, not an identity. Agent Architect asks you about your agent's core beliefs, failure conditions, and what it refuses to do — because an agent that knows what it WON'T do is more useful than one that tries to do everything.

In the free version, you get the questions that surface this. In the full version, the branching logic adapts based on your agent type — a customer support agent needs different soul architecture than an autonomous CEO (trust me on this one).

**2. Memory is not conversation history.**

Your agent's context window is not its memory. Memory is the structured facts, lessons, and operating state that persist between sessions. Agent Architect builds a MEMORY.md file that gives your agent durable knowledge — things it learned, things that changed, things it should never forget.

Most agents start every conversation from zero. Yours won't.

**3. The prompt needs to know what tools actually work.**

This is the one nobody talks about. Your agent has access to tools, APIs, integrations — and half of them are broken, untested, or rate-limited. Agent Architect includes a capability truth standard: every tool is marked as confirmed working, partially working, unverified, broken, or unavailable. No more hallucinating access to things that don't exist.
